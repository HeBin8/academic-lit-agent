Digital Twin-Driven Personalized Federated Learning with Hypernetworks and Low-Rank Adaptation

Abstract — With the rapid development of distributed intelligent technologies, federated learning and digital twin systems have increasingly supported cyber-physical services.

I. Introduction

Modern CPS environments face heterogeneous data, unstable edge resources, and strict privacy constraints. Personalized federated learning provides an effective path, but training efficiency and adaptation remain challenging.

II. Proposed Method

The paper introduces a hypernetwork-driven architecture with low-rank adaptation to generate personalized model parameters for heterogeneous clients.

III. Experiments

Results show improvements in classification accuracy and communication efficiency under non-IID settings.
